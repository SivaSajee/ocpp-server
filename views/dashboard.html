What is DLB?

Dynamic Load Balancing automatically adjusts your EV charging speed based on available power, solar generation, and household consumption.

⚡ PV Dynamic Balance

What it does: Charges your EV using excess solar power.

Best for: Daytime charging when the sun is shining, maximizing solar self-consumption.

Example:

Solar: 8kW, Home: 3kW → Charges at ~5kW (using excess solar)

∞ Extreme Mode

What it does: Charges at maximum speed (32A / 7.4kW) regardless of solar or grid conditions.

Best for: Emergency charging when you need the car ready ASAP.

⚠️ Note: Always charges at full power, even if importing from grid. Can be expensive!

🌙 Night Full Speed

What it does: Automatically switches to maximum charging speed during nighttime hours (22:00 - 06:00).

Best for: Taking advantage of cheaper night electricity rates.

Example:

10:00 AM → PV mode (solar), 11:00 PM → 32A (night rate)

🏠 Anti Overload

What it does: Prevents your home's electrical system from overloading by monitoring total power consumption.

Best for: Homes with limited electrical capacity, preventing circuit breaker trips.

✅ Safety: This mode acts as a "ceiling" - it runs alongside other modes to prevent overload.

🎯 Recommended Combinations

PV Dynamic + Anti Overload

Best for most users with solar panels

PV Dynamic + Night Full Speed + Anti Overload

Solar during day, fast charging at night

Extreme Mode + Anti Overload

Emergency charging with safety

🔍 Troubleshooting

Charging is too slow?

• Check if PV Dynamic is enabled - it limits to available solar
• Try Extreme Mode for maximum speed

Charger keeps stopping/starting?

• Solar may be fluctuating below 6A minimum
• Disable PV Dynamic or wait for better sun

Anti Overload always triggering?

• Reduce other household loads while charging
• Your main fuse size may be too small

💡 Quick Decision Guide

Have solar? → PV Dynamic + Anti Overload

No solar? → Night Full Speed + Anti Overload

Emergency? → Extreme Mode + Anti Overload

Not sure? → Enable only Anti Overload (safe default)
